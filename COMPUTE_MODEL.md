# REID Compute Model
### How REID treats compute as a flow and optimizes reasoning

The Compute Model is REID’s framework for understanding compute as a
structural flow resource. It ensures that reasoning remains efficient,
scalable, and aligned with both human cognition and cloud‑scale systems.

---

## 1. Compute as a Flow
In REID, compute is treated the same way as any other flow:
- Time  
- Energy  
- Attention  
- Money  
- Information  

Compute is not infinite.  
It must be allocated, conserved, and optimized.

---

## 2. Flow Constraints
Every reasoning process has compute constraints:
- Limited time  
- Limited attention  
- Limited hardware resources  
- Limited cognitive bandwidth  

REID models these constraints explicitly.

---

## 3. Mode T as Compute Optimization
Mode T is the compute‑efficient reasoning mode.

It reduces:
- Chain‑of‑thought length  
- Redundant loops  
- Over‑expansion  
- Drift  
- Cognitive load  

Mode T is the shortest structural path through the invariants.

---

## 4. Structural Compression
REID compresses reasoning by:
- Removing non‑structural details  
- Prioritizing invariants  
- Ranking forces  
- Fixing the horizon  
- Minimizing steps  

Compression increases clarity and reduces compute cost.

---

## 5. Compute‑Aware Force Ranking
Forces are ranked not only by importance but by compute impact.

High‑compute forces include:
- Ambiguity  
- Conflicting goals  
- Unbounded horizons  
- Excessive domain detail  

Mode T reduces or eliminates these forces.

---

## 6. Flow Bottlenecks
REID identifies compute bottlenecks such as:
- Too many branches  
- Too much recursion  
- Too much domain noise  
- Too many horizon shifts  

Then it restructures reasoning to bypass or compress them.

---

## 7. Cloud‑Scale Alignment
The Compute Model aligns naturally with:
- Azure resource management  
- Distributed compute  
- Parallel reasoning  
- Cost‑aware workloads  
- Latency‑sensitive systems  

REID is designed to scale from human reasoning to cloud reasoning.

---

## 8. Human Compute
Humans also have compute limits:
- Fatigue  
- Overwhelm  
- Limited
