# Forces Overview
### Understanding forces as structural pressures in the REID Cognitive Architecture

Forces are one of the four core invariants of REID.  
They represent the pressures, constraints, motivations, and influences
acting on a system, a human, or a domain. Forces determine how state
changes, how horizons shift, and how flows behave.

This document explains what forces are, how they operate, and why they
matter.

---

## 1. What Forces Are
Forces are **pressures acting on a system**.

Examples include:
- Deadlines  
- Expectations  
- Constraints  
- Motivations  
- Emotional pressures  
- Market pressures  
- Technical limitations  
- Social dynamics  
- Environmental conditions  

Forces push, pull, block, accelerate, or redirect movement.

---

## 2. Why Forces Matter
Forces determine:
- What direction a system moves  
- How fast it moves  
- What obstacles it faces  
- What opportunities exist  
- How horizons evolve  

Without forces, nothing changes.

---

## 3. Types of Forces

### **A. Internal Forces**
Originating inside the system:
- Motivation  
- Fatigue  
- Internal policies  
- Personal goals  
- Emotional state  

### **B. External Forces**
Originating outside the system:
- Deadlines  
- Market conditions  
- Social expectations  
- Environmental constraints  
- Other humans  

### **C. Structural Forces**
Built into the system:
- Architecture  
- Rules  
- Physical limits  
- Organizational design  

### **D. Emergent Forces**
Arising from interactions:
- Group dynamics  
- Feedback loops  
- Cultural norms  
- Systemic pressures  

---

## 4. Force Strength
Forces vary in intensity.

A strong force:
- Overrides weaker forces  
- Redirects flows  
- Changes horizons  
- Alters state quickly  

A weak force:
- Nudges  
- Influences  
- Modifies behavior slowly  

Mode T ranks forces by structural importance.

---

## 5. Force Direction
Forces have direction:
- Toward the horizon  
- Away from the horizon  
- Perpendicular to the horizon  
- Opposing other forces  

Direction determines whether a force is:
- Helpful  
- Harmful  
- Neutral  
- Distracting
