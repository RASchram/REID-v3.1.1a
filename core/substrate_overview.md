# Substrate Overview
### The foundational structural layer of the REID Cognitive Architecture

The Substrate is the deepest layer of REID.  
It is the shared structural foundation that all reasoning, translation,
and multi‑human modeling rests on. The substrate is domain‑agnostic,
stable, and invariant across contexts.

This document explains what the substrate is, why it matters, and how it
supports the entire REID architecture.

---

## 1. What the Substrate Is
The Substrate is the **structural layer beneath all domains**.

It contains:
- The invariants (state, forces, horizon, flows)  
- The relationships between them  
- The rules of structural reasoning  
- The patterns that appear across all systems  

The substrate is not content.  
It is **structure**.

---

## 2. Why the Substrate Exists
Domains change.  
Language changes.  
Contexts shift.  
Human perspectives differ.

But structure persists.

The substrate provides:
- Stability  
- Universality  
- Drift resistance  
- Cross‑domain clarity  
- A shared reasoning foundation  

It is the anchor of REID.

---

## 3. Invariants as Substrate Elements
The four invariants are the core components of the substrate:

- **State** — what exists now  
- **Forces** — what acts on it  
- **Horizon** — where it is going  
- **Flows** — what moves through it  

These invariants appear in every domain, which is why REID can reason
across domains without distortion.

---

## 4. Structural Relationships
The substrate defines the relationships between invariants:

- Forces act on state  
- Horizons shape forces  
- Flows enable or constrain movement  
- State changes when forces and flows interact  

These relationships are universal.

---

## 5. Domain Independence
The substrate does not depend on:
- Vocabulary  
- Culture  
- Expertise  
- Context  
- Domain knowledge  

This makes REID:
- Universal  
- Stable  
- Scalable  
- Easy to teach  
- Easy to translate  

The substrate is the reason REID works everywhere.

---

## 6. Substrate and Mode T
Mode T uses the substrate directly.

It:
- Removes domain noise  
- Compresses reasoning  
- Anchors to invariants  
- Minimizes compute  
- Prevents drift  

Mode T is the substrate expressed in compressed form.

---

## 7. Substrate and Multi‑Human Reasoning
The substrate allows multiple humans to be modeled structurally.

Each human has:
- A state  
- Forces  
- A horizon
