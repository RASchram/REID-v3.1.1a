Multi‑Node Reasoning Examples
How REID Operates Across Multiple Humans, Roles, and Perspectives
The multi‑human node model allows REID to reason across families,
teams, organizations, and any system involving more than one person.
This document provides examples of how REID maintains coherence
across multiple nodes while preserving individual horizons.

1. Example: Family Decision with Multiple Horizons
Scenario
A family is deciding whether to move to a new location.

Traditional reasoning often becomes:

Fragmented

Emotional

Repetitive

Misaligned

Hard to compare

REID Multi‑Node Structure
Node A (Parent 1)

Horizon: long‑term stability

Forces: job pressure, financial constraints

Flows: time, energy, money

Node B (Parent 2)

Horizon: emotional well‑being

Forces: stress, social ties

Flows: attention, support

Node C (Child)

Horizon: school continuity

Forces: social pressure

Flows: time, learning

REID Output
Shared substrate aligns structure

Individual horizons remain intact

Forces and flows are compared structurally

Decision emerges from invariant alignment

The family gains clarity without losing individuality.

Mapped invariants:
