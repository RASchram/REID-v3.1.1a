# REID Design Principles
### Foundational principles behind the REID Cognitive Architecture

REID is built on a set of structural, cognitive, and philosophical
principles that ensure clarity, stability, and cross‑domain reliability.
These principles guide every document, example, and extension of REID.

---

## 1. Structure Over Content
REID prioritizes **structure** (invariants) over domain content.
This allows reasoning to remain stable even when the domain changes.

Content varies.  
Structure persists.

---

## 2. Invariants as the Core Lens
All reasoning in REID flows through four invariants:

- **State** — what exists now  
- **Forces** — what is acting on it  
- **Horizon** — where it is going  
- **Flows** — what resources move through it  

These invariants appear in every domain, making REID universal.

---

## 3. Cross‑Domain Translation
REID is designed to translate reasoning between domains without drift.

The structure stays constant.  
Only the domain language changes.

This prevents confusion, misalignment, and overfitting to a single field.

---

## 4. Multi‑Human Alignment
REID models each human as a node with their own:
- State  
- Forces  
- Horizon  
- Flows  

This allows alignment without forcing agreement.

REID is not about consensus — it is about clarity.

---

## 5. Compute Efficiency
REID treats compute as a **flow resource**.

This leads to:
- Shorter reasoning chains  
- Fewer loops  
- Less drift  
- More clarity  
- Lower cost  

Mode T (tight mode) is the embodiment of this principle.

---

## 6. Long‑Arc Reasoning
REID is built for long‑arc planning:
- Multi‑step  
- Multi‑domain  
- Multi‑human  
- Multi‑horizon  

It avoids short‑term noise and focuses on structural trajectories.

---

## 7. Minimalism and Compression
REID favors:
- Fewer steps  
- Clearer structure  
- Compressed reasoning  
- Reduced cognitive load  

This makes it easier for humans and machines to use.

---

## 8. Integration, Not Replacement
REID does not replace tools or systems.

It integrates with:
- Microsoft ecosystem  
- Cloud compute  
- Human workflows  
- Organizational structures  

REID is a cognitive layer, not a competing system.

---

## 9. Transparency and Explainability
Every REID output should be:
- Traceable  
- Structural  
- Explainable  
- Reconstructable  

No black boxes.  
No hidden logic.

---

## 10. Stability Over Time
REID is designed to remain stable as:
- Domains evolve  
- Tools change  
- Teams shift  
- Contexts expand  

The invariants anchor the system.

---

These principles guide the evolution of REID and ensure that the
architecture remains coherent, scalable, and useful across domains.
